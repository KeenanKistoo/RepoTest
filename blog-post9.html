Reading: The Road to Technocolonialism - Giorgos Verdi

“Technocolonialism” refers to the process of powerful technological countries and companies who use their resources and advancements to gain power and control over countries (Verdi, 2020). The USA and China have already begun distributing their resources to Southern African countries (Verdi, 2020).

South Africa is a developing country with a high unemployment rate. Yes, artificial intelligence will not only create a more efficient economy but will also reduce/remove the factor of human error. Now, the main question is: Will this actually be beneficial in a developing country like South Africa? Personally, being someone who has access to technology, and would like to believe they are technologically efficient, I do see the benefits. For example, many, if not all, South African citizens have experienced standing in line with countless individuals; be it home affairs, or licencing departments. The systems are not efficient enough to handle so many people every single day. So yes, the improvement that AI would provide would obviously be helpful however, I do believe that it is important that the technological advancements that are put in place to control sensitive information such as identification numbers, passport numbers, etc. should be developed within our country. Firstly, this creates job opportunities for the citizens of South Africa and secondly, if the information is leaked, or handled incorrectly, the country will be able to find out how, why and to what extent the information was leaked or mishandled. Realistically speaking, if we put the power of information handling in the hands of these powerful countries, we cannot hold them accountable for mishandling it. Our country does not have the military capabilities to go up against these countries nor do we have the technological advancements to protect ourselves as they will have all the power. By placing them in control of our data and infrastructure we are only giving them more power over us.

At a more elementary level, with the high unemployment rates, we cannot afford to replace more citizens with AI. This will drastically affect our economy in the long run as we will have several individuals within certain job sectors who are unemployed. To develop more as a country, we cannot try to take shortcuts or sign contracts that we are not in full control of. Several parts of Africa are still recovering from the effects of The Scramble for Africa (colonialism). During that era, we did not have a say, and the land was just taken aggressively, however, in the current era, we have the ability to at least resist these powerful countries.
