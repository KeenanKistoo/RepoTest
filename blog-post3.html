Reflections On The Reading And The Lecture

Conventions refer to "shared practices" (Mailloux, 1983: 2). The idea or term that I tend to use when describing convention is mutual understanding. In terms of UI and UX, I have always believed that there are several boundaries that you need to stay within. In most design practices, there are always exceptions, however, it is important to differentiate between a bold design choice and just implementing something confusing and unnecessary. For example, we are accustomed to clicking on a 'burger icon' when looking for a menu. If changing this entirely and making the icon a link to the homepage would be confusing for users. When designing the interface for a website, it is important to put the website's ease of use before the aesthetics. When a website looks good but works poorly, users are more likely to close the website and find one more efficient to their needs.
