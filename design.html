Goal and Aesthtic

My goal was to create a minimalistic website that gives an impression of a digital journal. journal impression.

Colour Palette

In my first submission, I used very natural colours (black, white, beige and grey). I was struggling to match and break colours and my initial text was not particularly distinguishable from my backgrounds. Therefore, I opted for more natural tones to ensure better readability.

The colour palette illustrates the main colour choices for my website. The darker blues and teal represents the types of colours that I wear. As I look around my room and type this, I see at least 4 out of the 6 colours on the colour palette. I have also used shades of yellow, orange and red to break the monochrome look.

Background and Font

There were several changes with my font and background between each submission. The image below, is the background image that I used across my website for my first submission. At the time, I thought it gave a paper like aesthetic but now, I think it just ostentatious and uneccessary. In the image it may now be the clearest however, when coded to cover the entire page, the texture was very overpowering.

The background that you are currently looking at is a plain seashell tone. I felt this colour read particularly well and was not too dark or too bright. It also allowed for the dark teal colours to be read easily.

I have used the Google font: Gelasio. It is a variation of Times New Roman. It creates an elegant look while ensuring that all text can be read easily.

IxD Process:

1) Goal Alignment

The overall goal is to create a website that allows the user to easily navigate through. To achieve this, I would need to ensure that every interactive element is clearly displayed on the website, there is a clear loop where users can get to and from wherever they need to, and the website communicates to the user where they are on the website. I have kept a consistent navigation bar to allow users to navigate to and from different parts of my website easily.

2) Information Structure and User Flow

The flowchart is a very basic illustration on how a user can navigate through my website. It shows the continous loop between pages but also shows that the user can only veiw the blog posts by clicking on the blog section. From the blog section, the user has the option to navigate to any other page of the website.

Interface Elements

This is discussed across my design section. I tried to create a very simple website to ensure that my user remains in control throughout. In my blog section, the user has filter options which allow them to view only what they want to view. Since the blog section was a major part of this assignment, I tried my best to focus on it and throw as many ease of use buttons, filters, etc. as I could.
